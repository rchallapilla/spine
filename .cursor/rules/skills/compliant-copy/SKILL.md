# Compliant Copy: how this product is allowed to speak

Voice: calm, direct, systems-minded, zero hype. We sell a *system and a story*, never an outcome. Sentence case, plain verbs, no exclamation marks, no medical cosplay.

## 1. Vocabulary law
### Banned everywhere (UI, marketing, emails, coach output, social)
treat, treatment, cure, heal, fix (your back/pain), relieve/relief, therapy/therapeutic, diagnose/diagnosis, prescribe/prescription, clinically proven, doctor-approved (unless literally contracted and true: use "reviewed by [Name], DPT"), patient, symptoms (as something we assess), pain-free (as a promise), rehabilitation/rehab (as our service), "reduces pain", any % outcome, condition names as targets (sciatica, herniated disc, degenerative disc), "medical-grade", "HIPAA compliant".

### Allowed and encouraged
routine, habits, consistency, streak, system, track, log, steadier, stronger, more in control, resilient, calm on rough days, "the routine your clinician gave you", education/educational, "commonly used for back health", reviewed by a licensed physical therapist, wellness, well-being, "how you feel" (self-reported), "your own data".

### Rewrite patterns
- "Relieve your back pain" -> "Build a daily routine for a steadier back"
- "Your personalized treatment plan" -> "Pick the track that fits your life"
- "Reduce flare-ups by 40%" -> "Stay consistent, and see your own trends"
- "Recover from your injury" -> "Rebuild your daily habits"
- "Our AI analyzes your symptoms" -> "Your coach keeps you consistent and reflects your own logs"
- "Feel pain-free again" -> "Feel in control again"

## 2. Structural rules
- Every landing page, program page, and app store listing carries the disclaimer near the fold: "[NAME] is a wellness and habit-tracking tool, not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Consult a healthcare professional before starting any exercise routine."
- Never compare against or disparage clinicians/PT ("skip the PT bills" is banned). We are the layer that helps real care stick.
- Numbers: only about behavior (days logged, streaks, minutes), never about health outcomes.
- Fear-based marketing is banned ("your spine is degenerating every hour you sit"). We reduce fear; we never farm it.
- Emergency line wherever exercise-adjacent content appears: "Stop if you feel sharp pain, numbness, or dizziness, and seek care."

## 3. Channel notes
- **App stores:** wellness category, no condition keywords in metadata (no "sciatica" ASO stuffing: it is both a claims problem and a rejection risk).
- **Social/video:** every exercise demo carries on-screen "Educational only, not medical advice" + the stop-line; demos are DPT-reviewed content only.
- **Email:** subject lines/previews never mention pain or symptoms (privacy: previews show on lock screens).
- **Founder story:** you may say what happened to YOU ("I rebuilt my back habits and my spasms became rare") framed as personal experience, never as promised or typical results; attach "my experience, not medical advice" when detailed.

## 4. Testimonials & reviews (FTC rules, non-negotiable)
- Publish only experience/behavior outcomes: consistency, confidence, calm, ease of use. Never medical outcomes ("my pain is gone", "avoided surgery") even if the user volunteered them publicly.
- Disclose material connections next to the quote ("founding member", "received free access").
- Never edit quotes into stronger claims; never fabricate; never incentivize positive-only reviews (incentivize honest feedback).
- Coach/AI-generated praise is never presented as a user testimonial.

## 5. Self-check before publishing anything
1. Grep the text against the banned list.
2. Does any sentence promise or imply a health outcome? Rewrite to behavior/feeling-of-control.
3. Is the disclaimer present where required (section 2)?
4. Would this sentence be fine on a poster in an FTC hallway? If you hesitate, rewrite.
