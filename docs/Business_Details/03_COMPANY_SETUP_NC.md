# COMPANY SETUP: North Carolina, step by step

Do this in Phase B, after Gate A passes and AFTER the Wells Fargo OBA disclosure (02_LEGAL section 10).

## 1. Entity choice: single-member NC LLC (not Delaware)
Delaware C-corp exists for VC fundraising. You are bootstrapping a solo wellness SaaS; an NC LLC gives liability protection, pass-through taxes, and minimal ceremony. Convert later if you ever raise; conversion is routine.

## 2. Formation checklist (one afternoon, ~$125-260)
1. Name cleared (01_STRATEGY section 5) and includes "LLC".
2. Optional: reserve name, $10-30, only if filing later.
3. File Articles of Organization online at sosnc.gov: $125 one-time filing fee to the NC Secretary of State.
4. Registered agent: you can be your own (free) but your street address becomes public record; a commercial agent (~$100-200/yr) keeps your home address private. Hiring a service typically costs between $199 and $400 per year, though budget options run ~$125.
5. Operating agreement: single-member template (Northwest/LLC University free templates are fine); sign it, keep it with company records. Banks and courts respect LLCs that respect themselves.
6. EIN: free, 10 minutes, irs.gov directly (never pay a third party for an EIN).

## 3. Ongoing NC obligations
- Annual report due each year by April 15, $200 by mail or $203 online, starting the year after your Articles of Organization are approved. Calendar it now; NC sends a notice and you get 60 days before administrative dissolution.
- Keep registered agent info current.

## 4. Money hygiene (day one, non-negotiable)
- Business checking (Mercury or Relay: free, online, startup-friendly) + a business card used for every expense.
- Bookkeeping: Wave (free) or QuickBooks Simple Start from the first dollar. Categories: software, legal/professional, insurance, marketing, contractor (DPT), fees.
- Never commingle. Pay yourself via owner's draw transfers, documented.

## 5. Taxes (single-member LLC = disregarded entity)
- Federal: profits flow to your 1040 Schedule C; self-employment tax 15.3% on net profit on top of income tax. File quarterly estimated taxes (Form 1040-ES) once profitable; penalty avoidance matters more than precision.
- NC: flat individual income tax on the pass-through profit; no separate LLC-level income tax for disregarded entities, and most LLCs taxed as pass-throughs won't owe NC franchise tax.
- **Sales tax on SaaS in NC and other states:** nuanced and state-by-state (some states tax SaaS, some do not, and rules shift). Do not hand-roll this: turn on **Stripe Tax** from the first sale; it calculates, collects, and reports by jurisdiction. Confirm NC registration needs with a CPA at your first $10k of revenue.
- Hire a CPA for the first business tax year (~$400-800). Cheap versus one mistake.
- **S-corp election:** ignore until sustained net profit ~$60-80k+/yr; then it can save on self-employment tax at the cost of payroll ceremony. A CPA decision, later.

## 6. Insurance (before first beta user)
General liability + tech E&O + cyber bundle: quotes from Hiscox, Next Insurance, Vouch, Embroker. Expect ~$600-1,200/yr at your size. When you later add exercise video content or coaching calls, tell the carrier; misdescribed operations void coverage.

## 7. Records folder (create today, digital)
Articles + stamped approval, operating agreement, EIN letter, OBA approval from employer, insurance policies, attorney engagement + reviewed docs, DPT contract + review memo, annual report receipts, trademark filing. One folder; future-you and any acquirer will thank you.

## 8. What NOT to spend on now
Delaware anything, "LLC formation packages" beyond the state fee, paid EIN services, a trademark before Gate A, an accountant on retainer, an office, inventory of merch. Every early dollar goes to: attorney review, insurance, DPT, and reaching users.
