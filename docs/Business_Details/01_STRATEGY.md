# STRATEGY: market, wedge, clinic playbook

## 1. Market reality
**Consumer back apps are a graveyard for solos:** Hinge/Sword own B2B clinical, Curable-class apps own consumer mindshare, YouTube is free. You do not out-market them at 8 hrs/wk.
**The clinic-side gap is real:** home-exercise-program (HEP) adherence is a known, named failure point in PT. Incumbent HEP tools (Medbridge HEP, Physitrack, PT Everywhere, WebPT's HEP) are exercise-PDF-and-video libraries with logging bolted on: weak on daily habit design, no free-text logging, no AI adherence coach, no longitudinal risk flags. Patients stop using them by week 2, which is exactly the problem.

## 2. The wedge
**Product:** the adherence layer for structured recovery. The clinician authors the program and clinical context in the product; the patient gets a genuinely pleasant daily app (spine widget, quick-log, flare mode, guide); the clinic gets an adherence dashboard and weekly digest between visits.
**Moat:** the program-to-product pipeline: clinician-authored protocol -> structured habits, milestones, guides, and coach guardrails -> LLM removes friction (free-text logging) and synthesizes (weekly reports) without ever improvising medically. Plus a founder who is his own case study, with real longitudinal data in the demo.
**Legal posture:** strongest available: a licensed clinician authors the individualized content; the app is delivery and adherence (02_LEGAL sections 3-4b).

## 3. What clinics actually buy (speak to these, not features)
- **Plan-of-care completion and retention:** patients who do the home program come back, finish care, refer.
- **Differentiation:** independent and cash-based practices sell an experience; "your program, in an app, with weekly summaries" is marketable.
- **Time:** the digest replaces the "so... did you do your exercises?" interrogation.
Objections you will hear, and answers: "another app" (patients get one free simple app, no portal login); "my patients won't use it" (that is the pilot's success metric, and the founder used it 200+ days); "EMR integration?" (year-2 conversation; pilot is standalone by design); "liability?" (clinician authors content, guardrails and red-flag escalation built in, attorney-reviewed terms).

## 4. Clinic ICP and list building
Independent PT, chiro, and sports-med practices, 1-5 clinicians, owner-operated, cash or hybrid payer mix (they buy differentiation), active on social (they market, so they invest in retention), Charlotte metro first. Build 40-50: Google Maps, NC physical therapy association directory, Instagram local fitness/rehab scene. Disqualify: hospital systems, high-volume insurance mills (no budget authority, long cycles).

## 5. Outreach and meeting flow
**Email/DM script:** "I'm a product manager who rebuilt my own back with a structured program, and I built an app that turned my PT's plan into daily habits, trends, and a weekly AI summary with clinician-set guardrails. I'm selecting 3 Charlotte-area pilot clinics. 20 minutes to show you my real data?"
**Meeting (20 min):** 5 min their world (how do you assign home programs today? how do you know if patients do them?), 10 min demo with your own data ending on the clinician editor and dashboard mock, 5 min pilot terms and the LOI ask. Never oversell the AI; sell adherence visibility.
**The ask:** a signed pilot LOI on the spot or within the week.

## 6. Pilot LOI outline (attorney reviews before first signature; ask #7 in 02_LEGAL)
Free 90-day pilot, up to 20 patients; clinic provides biweekly 20-min feedback calls and a testimonial if satisfied; written success criteria (e.g., 60%+ weekly patient adherence, clinician time saved); stated post-pilot price $149/mo (founding clinics locked at $99/mo for 12 months); either party exits on 2 weeks notice; clinic owns its program content, you own the platform; data handling per the adherence-only scope (02_LEGAL 4b).

## 7. One relationship rule
Your own PT is a natural design partner AFTER your care relationship is established, with intent disclosed plainly. Never blend selling into a treatment session; ask at the end of the episode of care.

## 8. Naming (unchanged process, B2B lens)
"Spine" alone will not clear. Generate 15 candidates with Fable 5 (two syllables, spellable, evokes steadiness/backbone/routine, credible in a clinic lobby). Knockout: USPTO classes 9 and 44, Google, app stores, domain, handles. Gut-check survivors with 3 clinicians from outreach. File the trademark after Gate A passes.

## 9. Pre-mortem
- You build clinic features before an LOI exists (Gate A forbids it).
- A pilot clinic's patients don't activate: countermeasure is in the pilot design: clinician introduces the app in-session, co-branded onboarding one-pager, week-1 adherence review call.
- Copy drift into medical claims burns trust (skills enforce).
- Founder calendar collapse: 3 meetings/wk cap and the roadmap's kill criteria are the honest answer.

## Appendix: B2C fallback track (activate ONLY if Gate A fails B2B but passes consumer)
**Wedge:** recovery habit OS for desk-bound professionals with recurring back trouble; founder-story-led. **ICP:** 30-50, desk job 8+ hrs, $80k+ household, athletic identity, already saw a PT/doctor once, pays for Whoop/Strava/Calm. **Positioning:** the daily companion that turns your clinician's advice into an automatic routine; not medical care, proud of it. **Validation interview script:** walk me through your last episode; what do you do to prevent the next one, honestly; what have you paid for; price reaction to 3 mock screens ("would you pay that today?" then silence); what makes this a no. **Anti-ICP routed away by the intake screener:** acute severe pain, post-surgical, radiating symptoms, diagnosis-seekers.
