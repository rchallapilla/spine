# MARKETING & GTM: founder-led clinic sales

Your unfair advantages: a true, documented founder story; a live app full of your own longitudinal data (the demo); enterprise-grade PM skills. Your constraints: ~7-8 hrs/wk around a W-2 job, and a compliance line every public word must respect (skills/compliant-copy governs everything, including LinkedIn posts and clinic one-pagers).

## 1. The story spine (opens clinic doors and consumer hearts alike)
"Athletic guy, 43, desk job. Back kept going out. I reviewed my own case like a product problem, found the real drivers, built a system, rebuilt my back, and logged every day of it. Clinicians kept telling me the hard part isn't the program, it's getting patients to do it. So I turned the system into something clinics can run." Never claims cure or treatment; claims a system, a story, and adherence.

## 2. The sales motion (this IS the marketing for 6 months)
- **List:** 40-50 qualified clinics (criteria in 01_STRATEGY section 4). Maintain it in a simple sheet: name, owner, channel, last touch, stage.
- **Cadence:** 10 outreach touches/week (email + LinkedIn DM; script in 01 section 5). Follow-up rhythm: day 4 nudge, day 10 value follow-up (send the case-study one-pager or a relevant post), day 21 close-the-loop.
- **Meetings:** cap 3/week, offered at 7:00am, lunch, or 5:30pm. Every meeting ends with the LOI ask or a clear no.
- **Funnel math to expect:** 50 touches -> ~10 meetings -> 3-5 interested -> 2-3 LOIs. If 20 conversations produce zero LOIs, the kill criteria in 00_ROADMAP apply.

## 3. Sales collateral (make once, in Cursor, one evening each)
1. **One-page leave-behind (PDF):** the story, 3 app screenshots, the dashboard mock, pilot terms. Compliant-copy reviewed.
2. **Patient onboarding one-pager (co-brandable):** "Your clinic runs your program in [NAME]": QR to install, what it does, what it never does (not medical advice line).
3. **The demo:** your live app, rehearsed as a 10-minute walkthrough ending on the clinician editor and dashboard.

## 4. Proof engine (starts during pilots)
- Biweekly pilot feedback calls double as case-study material.
- Case study #1 (the growth asset): adherence percentages, activation numbers, clinician workflow quotes. FTC-safe rule: behavior and workflow outcomes only ("patients logged 71% of days", "saves me the did-you-do-it conversation"), never patient medical outcomes, incentives disclosed.
- Referral ask is explicit at conversion: "which two practice owners should see this?"

## 5. Light content layer (keeps the consumer option warm, 1-2 hrs/wk max)
- LinkedIn 1 post/week: build-in-public, pilot lessons (anonymized), habit-design thinking. Your PM network contains both future clinic intros and future consumers.
- The consumer landing page + waitlist stays live; waitlist gets one honest email/month.
- No SEO engine, no shorts pipeline, no Product Hunt: those belong to the fallback appendix and activate only per Gate A.

## 6. Lifecycle email (minimal, per saas-foundations)
Pilot-phase only: patient onboarding D0/D2/D7, activation nudge D3-if-no-logs, weekly patient report, weekly clinic digest. Subject lines never reference pain or symptoms (lock-screen privacy + compliant-copy).

## 7. KPI review (15 min, Friday)
Touches -> meetings -> LOIs -> pilot patient activation (7 logs in first 14 days) -> weekly adherence -> conversion. One experiment per week against the weakest number. Run it like a product you'd ship at work.

## 8. Skip list (6 months)
Paid ads, PR, conferences, a podcast, brand redesigns, EMR integrations, any channel you cannot feed weekly. One motion done relentlessly beats five done occasionally.

## Appendix: consumer launch playbook (activate only per Gate A fallback)
Channels: LinkedIn build-in-public 2/wk + Reddit (r/backpain, r/Posture, r/desksetup) with the 10:1 value rule and mod pre-clearance. SEO clusters: desk-life routines, habit systems, founder-story serialization, tool comparisons; 2 posts/wk. Launch week: waitlist 3-email warmup (story, sneak peek, founding offer) -> Product Hunt with maker story -> pre-cleared Reddit posts -> Show HN (guardrail architecture angle) -> day-7 transparency recap. Goal: 1,000 visitors, 300 signups, 50-100 founding members. Lifecycle: onboarding D0/D2/D7, weekly report as the retention workhorse with share card, D30 win-back. Testimonials: experience and consistency outcomes only, incentives disclosed, never medical outcomes.
